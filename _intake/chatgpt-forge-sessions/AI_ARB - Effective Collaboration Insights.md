# The primitives I would define first

## 1. **Work Item**

A normalized unit of candidate reality.

Every meaningful idea becomes a Work Item.

Fields might include:

* title
* source conversation / source excerpt
* category
* problem statement
* candidate value
* adjacent temptation areas
* current scope
* status
* related docs / ADRs / repos
* implementation relevance
* closure state

This is the most important primitive.

Because once your thoughts become **addressable units**, you stop losing them to conversational entropy.

* * *

## 2. **Deliberation Packet**

A structured bundle sent to one or more models.

Contains:

* the Work Item
* relevant corpus excerpts
* current architecture constraints
* desired role (`critic`, `synthesizer`, `grounder`, etc.)
* required output format
* anti-sprawl instructions

This prevents every model interaction from starting from scratch.

It also creates repeatability.

This is the difference between “chatting with AI” and “running a design process.”

* * *

## 3. **Ratification Record**

A small formal record of what was actually decided.

Not every idea deserves an ADR.

But many ideas deserve a **decision artifact**.

This should capture:

* what was decided
* what was explicitly _not_ adopted
* why
* what remains open
* what would invalidate the decision later

This is crucial for you because you often revisit concept space over time.

Without decision memory, you risk re-litigating your own design every month.

That burns enormous cognitive energy.

* * *

## 4. **Execution Packet**

A machine-usable output for implementation or follow-up.

Examples:

* “Generate ADR patch”
* “Create repo issue set”
* “Draft implementation plan”
* “Produce service contract”
* “Generate review checklist”
* “Create test harness plan”

This is where “thought” becomes “work.”
